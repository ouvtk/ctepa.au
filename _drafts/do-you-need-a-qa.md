title: "You probably don't need a dedicated QA"
---

It is useful to have QA when work is close to impossible to automate (variety of devices and their uncompliance go up through the roof, like in the 'best times' of TVs). In many other cases by having a separate QA that helps team to keep quality bar higher you may doing a disservice. When there is no QA your team has to own the results, and all the outcome of the work they are doing. If you have a separate QA function, you may end up in a situation where half-assed features being passed 'over-the-fence' to QA folks. This will contribute to higher back-and-forth and higher stress.
